Research Interests

I am generally interested in Brain-inspired Artificial Intelligence， Social Cognition and Reinforcement Learning. My long-term goal is to build human-like artificial intelligence inspired by brain cognitive functions.

During my doctoral studies, my research focused on the topic of the brain-inspired Theory of Mind (ToM) spiking neural networks (SNNs) model. ToM is a crucial aspect of social cognition, which can inspire the development of more biologically plausible, interpretable, prosocial and empathetic artificial intelligence models. Drawing inspiration from the structure and mechanisms of ToM in the human brain, we constructed a multi-brain region collaborative ToM model using SNNs, zero-order and first-order ToM models, as well as collective ToM models which are capable of handling complex inference tasks. We found that agents equipped with a Theory of Mind (ToM) model could exhibit human-like behaviors, such as imagination and helping, across various tasks involving competition, cooperation, and even independent relationships, by understanding other agents.
